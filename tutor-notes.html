Tutor Notes – Number Cards Exercise

Dataset explanation:

The numbers shown in the exercise are the salaries of the New York Red Bulls soccer team from 2012.

Source: Capology – NY Red Bulls Salaries 2012 (last checked April 2025)

Key statistics:

  • Count (n): 28 players
  • Sum: Approx. $16,000,000
  • Mean (average): $564,798
  • Median: $101,248 (approximately $100,000) – falls between $90,000 and $112,496
  • Mode: $44,000 – the second-lowest salary. While this doesn't correspond to a minimum wage, it still reflects common lower-tier pay.

Workbook tabs overview:

  1. Raw Numbers:
    Contains only the salary figures. Participants use this sheet to make initial observations and visual patterns.
  2. Numbers + Categories:
    Each salary is paired with a category label. These labels represent player positions:
    • K = Goalkeeper
    • D = Defender
    • M = Midfielder
    • F = Forward
    This allows learners to explore whether player position correlates with pay.
  3. Summary Stats:
    Provides key numerical summaries including min, max, range, median, mode, mean, and totals — useful for cross-checking learner calculations or discussing distribution shape.
  4. Visuals:
    Includes pre-prepared box plots and histograms to help learners interpret spread, outliers, and skew in the data.

Pedagogical notes:

  • Emphasise that learners should avoid assumptions early on — the clues (discrete, exact, complete dataset) are designed to encourage disciplined thinking.
  • There are multiple plausible hypotheses, but only one correct interpretation. Encourage discussion around alternative ideas and how data supports or refutes them.
  • Look out for misconceptions around the mode or assuming the dataset is incomplete or rounded.
  • When the categories are revealed, prompt learners to explore possible biases, inequality, or structural patterns in pay by role.
